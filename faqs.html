Certificates of Analysis

A Certificate of Analysis is a lot-specific document issued by an accredited third-party laboratory that verifies the identity, purity, and quality of a given compound. Each CoA includes results from analytical testing methods such as HPLC, mass spectrometry, endotoxin testing, and microbial screening.

Our CoAs are issued per lot, meaning the document corresponds to the exact batch of product you receive — not a generic or historical result.

Your lot number is printed on the product label. Visit our Certificates page, enter your lot number, and download the corresponding CoA. If you cannot locate your CoA, contact our support team and we will provide it directly.

We exclusively use U.S.-based, ISO/IEC 17025:2017-accredited third-party analytical laboratories. Sterility testing is performed by two independent labs per lot. We do not perform in-house testing on any compound we sell — all analytical data comes from accredited external sources.

Product Storage & Handling

Most lyophilized peptide compounds should be stored at -20°C (−4°F) in a sealed, moisture-free environment away from direct light. Once reconstituted, compounds should be refrigerated at 2–8°C and used within the timeframe noted on your CoA.

Specific storage instructions for each compound are included in the product documentation and on the product label. Always follow the guidance provided in your lot-specific CoA.

Shelf life varies by compound. Lyophilized (freeze-dried) peptides are generally stable for 24 months when stored correctly at -20°C. Expiration dates are printed on each product label and are supported by stability data documented in the CoA.

Reconstitution requirements vary by compound. Bacteriostatic water is commonly used for peptide reconstitution in research settings, though some compounds may require dilute acetic acid or another solvent for proper solubility. Always refer to the compound-specific documentation included with your order or consult your CoA for guidance.
